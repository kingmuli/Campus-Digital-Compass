Laptop Reviews from Students

Read honest reviews from fellow students about their laptop experiences

Dell XPS 13

Jackson Waweru

Business Information Technology, Meru University

★★★★★

The Dell XPS 13 has been perfect for my programming courses. The lightweight design makes it easy to carry around campus, and the battery lasts through all my classes. The screen is gorgeous and the keyboard is comfortable for long coding sessions.

Pros

  • Excellent build quality
  • Long battery life
  • Bright, colorful display

Cons

  • Expensive
  • Limited ports

Posted: March 15, 2024

MacBook Air M2

Shenan Mumbi

Biochemistry, Meru University

★★★★☆

As a biochemistry student, I needed a reliable laptop for research and data analysis. The M2 chip handles all my statistical software with ease. The only downside is some scientific software isn't available for macOS, so I occasionally need to use the computer lab.

Pros

  • Incredible performance
  • Silent operation (no fan)
  • Great battery life

Cons

  • Some software compatibility issues
  • Expensive to repair

Posted: February 28, 2024

Lenovo ThinkPad X1 Carbon

King Tonny Mulei Muli

Physics, Meru University

★★★★★

This laptop is a workhorse for physics simulations and data analysis. The keyboard is the best I've used on any laptop, which is great for writing long reports. It's survived being dropped twice and still works perfectly - very durable!

Pros

  • Extremely durable
  • Best keyboard on any laptop
  • Powerful for simulations

Cons

  • Expensive
  • Average webcam quality

Posted: January 10, 2024

HP Spectre x360

Praise Nyuthe

Mathematics, Meru University

★★★★☆

The 2-in-1 design is fantastic for math students - I can take notes by hand during lectures and then flip it to laptop mode for programming assignments. The OLED screen makes graphs and equations look crisp and clear.

Pros

  • Versatile 2-in-1 design
  • Beautiful OLED display
  • Good performance

Cons

  • Battery life could be better
  • Gets warm under heavy load

Posted: December 5, 2023

ASUS ZenBook 14 OLED

Arnold Ateya

Environmental Science, Meru University

★★★☆☆

Good value for money with the OLED display, but I've had some driver issues with scientific software. The screen is gorgeous for GIS work, but the battery drains quickly when running resource-intensive applications.

Pros

  • Affordable OLED option
  • Lightweight
  • Good keyboard

Cons

  • Driver compatibility issues
  • Mediocre battery life

Posted: November 20, 2023

Acer Swift 3

Meghan Adhiambo

Mechanical Engineering, Meru University

★★★☆☆

A decent budget option for chemistry students. It handles my molecular modeling software adequately, though sometimes lags with complex simulations. The build quality is good for the price, but the screen could be brighter.

Pros

  • Affordable
  • Good performance for price
  • Lightweight

Cons

  • Dim display
  • Plastic build feels cheap
